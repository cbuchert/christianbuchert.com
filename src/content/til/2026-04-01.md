Longform conversation with Claude primed with a rigorously crafted UX Designer system prompt that summarizes ideas and decisions with a clickable prototype is my current favorite way to work through mundane UX. Being able to navigate the design conceptually and architecturally and by use case and backed by data is such a natural fit. When interface elements get complex it seems to fall apart (lookin' at you, complex calendar implementations and bespoke data visualizations). In those cases Figma is pure gold.

A year and change ago I was asked to put together a presentation on where UX was headed with AI tooling. After some digging and some soul searching, I proposed two directions:

1. Current design tools continue to be used by serious teams doing serious work because the value of design work culminates in high fidelity mockup deliverables and the process (hypothesis, research, design, validate) doesn't fundamentally change. Expect design tools like Figma to modernize.
2. Interactive prototypes are built agentically in response to business requirements, product specification, product research, UX research, user vibe, etc. Someone has the difficult task of convincing Product that their generated interactive prototype is a design doc and not production code. The prototype becomes the engineering deliverable.

For my personal project use cases, I think that #2 is winning.
